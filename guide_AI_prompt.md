## 🧾 1. Basic Grammarly-Like Prompts

### 🧩 `/proofread`

**Effect:** Fixes grammar, punctuation, and typos only.
**Use when:** You want minimal edits — just correctness.
**Example Input:**

> /proofread This article discuss about how AI help people learn faster

**Output:**

> This article discusses how AI helps people learn faster.

---

### 🧩 `/grammar-check`

**Effect:** Checks grammar and gives explanations of corrections.
**Use when:** You want to *learn* from your mistakes.
**Example Input:**

> /grammar-check He don’t have no idea about the project.

**Output:**

> Correction: “He doesn’t have any idea about the project.”
> Explanation: “Don’t” changes to “doesn’t” for singular subjects; “no” changes to “any” to avoid double negatives.

---

## ✍️ 2. Style & Clarity Refinement Prompts

### 🧩 `/gpt-editor`

**Effect:** Acts like Grammarly Premium — improves grammar, tone, and readability while keeping your meaning.
**Use when:** You want writing to sound polished and professional.
**Example Input:**

> /gpt-editor Improve this paragraph: I am writing to let you know that the meeting is being postponed because of unavoidable reasons.

**Output:**

> I’m writing to inform you that the meeting has been postponed due to unforeseen circumstances.

---

### 🧩 `/clarify`

**Effect:** Makes sentences more direct and easy to read.
**Use when:** Writing feels wordy or confusing.
**Example Input:**

> /clarify Due to the fact that the project was delayed, the outcomes were not as expected.

**Output:**

> Because the project was delayed, the results didn’t meet expectations.

---

### 🧩 `/concise`

**Effect:** Removes unnecessary words — Grammarly’s “conciseness” tool.
**Use when:** Writing is repetitive or long-winded.
**Example Input:**

> /concise It is important to note that this report mainly focuses on the primary causes that led to the incident.

**Output:**

> This report focuses on the main causes of the incident.

---

## 🎯 3. Tone and Formality Control Prompts

### 🧩 `/professional`

**Effect:** Makes your writing sound polished and workplace-appropriate.
**Use when:** Writing emails, reports, or formal communication.
**Example Input:**

> /professional Can u pls send me the file asap?

**Output:**

> Could you please send me the file as soon as possible? Thank you.

---

### 🧩 `/formal`

**Effect:** Increases formality and removes slang.
**Use when:** Writing official letters, proposals, or essays.
**Example Input:**

> /formal The results were kinda weird, but we tried our best.

**Output:**

> The results were somewhat unexpected, but we made every effort to achieve accuracy.

---

### 🧩 `/natural`

**Effect:** Keeps the grammar correct but sounds more like human conversation — less robotic.
**Use when:** You want Grammarly’s “just right” tone.
**Example Input:**

> /natural Kindly note that the document was shared yesterday.

**Output:**

> Just a quick note — I shared the document yesterday.

---

## 💼 4. Advanced Grammarly+ Prompts (Pro-Level)

### 🧩 `/rephrase`

**Effect:** Rewrites sentences for better tone, rhythm, or structure.
**Use when:** Sentences feel stiff or awkward.
**Example Input:**

> /rephrase The solution which was proposed by the committee has been implemented successfully.

**Output:**

> The committee’s proposed solution has been successfully implemented.

---

### 🧩 `/tone-adjust`

**Effect:** You specify tone — the model rewrites to match it.
**Use when:** You need tone-perfect writing (email, blog, etc.).
**Example Input:**

> /tone-adjust polite Rewrite: “I need this report now.”

**Output:**

> Could you please send me the report at your earliest convenience?

---

### 🧩 `/rewrite like Grammarly Premium`

**Effect:** The full Grammarly treatment — grammar + flow + clarity + engagement.
**Use when:** You want everything polished — like a Premium rewrite.
**Example Input:**

> /rewrite like Grammarly Premium Improve: “The new system is better but has some problems that we need to solve soon.”

**Output:**

> The new system performs better overall, though several issues still need prompt resolution.

## 💡 Bonus Prompt: Context-Aware Editing

If you want to edit with purpose (Grammarly’s “goals” feature), use this:

> **Prompt:**
> “Edit this text for grammar, clarity, and tone. Target audience: university students. Goal: informative but friendly.”
> *(Then paste your text.)*

This way, the model adapts edits to match your *intent* — not just fix errors.
