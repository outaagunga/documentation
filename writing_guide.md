
# Writing a news story  
Here‚Äôs a clear step-by-step breakdown of the process ‚Äî from idea generation to publication:  

---

### üß† **1. Idea Generation**

**Goal:** Find a story worth telling.

* **Look for newsworthiness:** Think *timeliness, impact, proximity, conflict, novelty, prominence.*
* **Sources of ideas:**

  * Current events, press releases, social media, community meetings, public records.
  * Tips from readers or colleagues.
  * Trends, data, or human-interest angles.
* **Ask:** ‚ÄúWhy should readers care about this now?‚Äù

---

### üîé **2. Research & Backgrounding**

**Goal:** Understand the story before reporting it.

* Gather background info: previous coverage, official reports, data, and historical context.
* Identify key people, organizations, and documents involved.
* Learn enough to ask smart questions during interviews.

---

### üé§ **3. Reporting (Information Gathering)**

**Goal:** Collect facts, voices, and evidence.

* **Interviews:** Talk to multiple, credible sources (officials, witnesses, experts, affected people).
* **Observation:** Visit the scene, take notes on what you see, hear, and feel.
* **Documents & data:** Verify all statistics and quotes.
* **Record accurately:** Use notes, recordings, or both.

---

### üß© **4. Angle & Story Planning**

**Goal:** Decide how to tell the story.

* Identify your **angle** ‚Äî the central idea or focus of your piece.

  * Example: Instead of ‚Äúlocal bridge repairs,‚Äù your angle could be ‚Äúresidents frustrated over year-long bridge delays.‚Äù
* Choose the **story type:** breaking news, feature, investigative, profile, analysis, etc.
* Outline your structure: decide what information goes where.

---

### ‚úçÔ∏è **5. Writing the Story**

**Goal:** Craft a clear, accurate, and engaging article.

#### Typical Structure:

1. **Headline:** Short, accurate, and attention-grabbing.
2. **Lead (Lede):** First sentence/paragraph ‚Äî tells the most important fact or sums up the story.
3. **Nut Graph:** Explains why the story matters (often follows the lead).
4. **Body:** Provides details, quotes, and background in logical order (often using the *inverted pyramid* ‚Äî most important info first).
5. **Conclusion:** Wraps up with context or a forward-looking statement.

#### Writing Tips:

* Use clear, concise, factual language.
* Attribute all opinions and disputed facts.
* Avoid jargon and bias.
* Check names, titles, dates, and figures carefully.

---

### üïµÔ∏è **6. Fact-Checking & Editing**

**Goal:** Ensure accuracy, fairness, and clarity.

* Verify every detail and quote.
* Ensure balance (include multiple perspectives).
* Check grammar, spelling, and style (usually AP Style for news).
* Have an editor review for structure, flow, and tone.

---

### üßæ **7. Legal & Ethical Checks**

**Goal:** Avoid libel, invasion of privacy, and bias.

* Ensure all claims are supported by evidence.
* Avoid plagiarism; credit all sources.
* Confirm consent for sensitive material (photos, minors, victims, etc.).

---

### üåê **8. Publishing**

**Goal:** Deliver your story to readers effectively.

* Prepare digital elements: photos, videos, graphics, links, SEO keywords.
* Upload to the publication‚Äôs CMS (content management system).
* Coordinate with editors or producers for timing and promotion (social media, newsletters, etc.).
* Monitor audience feedback and correct errors promptly if necessary.

---

### üîÅ **9. Post-Publication Follow-Up**

**Goal:** Keep the story alive and accountable.

* Track developments and reader reactions.
* Publish updates or corrections if needed.
* Consider follow-up stories or deeper investigations.

---

# Logical flow patterns for major news types:

---

### üß≠ 1. **Hard News / Straight News**
**Purpose:** Report fresh facts quickly and objectively.
**Logical Flow:**
```
> Lead (who/what/where/when) ‚Üí Key facts (how/why) ‚Üí Official statements ‚Üí Context/background ‚Üí Next steps or conclusion
```
**Example Flow:**
Breaking: ‚ÄúPolice confirm flood damage‚Äù ‚Üí Details of the incident ‚Üí Quote from officials ‚Üí Historical flood pattern ‚Üí Ongoing response.

---

### üß© 2. **Feature / Human-Interest Story**
**Purpose:** Engage emotions, tell stories behind the facts.
**Logical Flow:**
```
> Narrative lead ‚Üí Character/story introduction ‚Üí Broader theme or issue ‚Üí Expert insight ‚Üí Wider implications ‚Üí Reflective or emotional ending
```
**Example Flow:**
Farmer‚Äôs struggle ‚Üí Drought impact ‚Üí Expert on climate ‚Üí Community adaptation ‚Üí Hopeful closing scene.

---

### üìä 3. **Trend or Data-Driven Story**
**Purpose:** Highlight emerging patterns or social shifts.
**Logical Flow:**
```
> Trend statement ‚Üí Supporting data/evidence ‚Üí Expert explanation ‚Üí Counterpoint or alternative view ‚Üí Broader social/economic context ‚Üí Concluding insight
```
---

### üß† 4. **Explainer / Backgrounder**
**Purpose:** Simplify complex topics for readers.
**Logical Flow:**
```
> Question or problem ‚Üí Historical background ‚Üí How it works / key terms ‚Üí Expert input ‚Üí Implications ‚Üí What happens next / takeaway
```
**Example Flow:**
Why fuel prices rise ‚Üí History of subsidy ‚Üí Supply chain explained ‚Üí Expert analysis ‚Üí Future projections ‚Üí Summary takeaway.

---

### üïµÔ∏è‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è 5. **Investigative Story**
**Purpose:** Expose hidden facts or wrongdoing.
**Logical Flow:**
```
> Hook or revelation ‚Üí Evidence and data ‚Üí Testimonies/interviews ‚Üí Denials or counterpoints ‚Üí Wider systemic pattern ‚Üí Call for accountability / resolution
```
**Example Flow:**
Leak reveals misuse ‚Üí Supporting documents ‚Üí Victim accounts ‚Üí Official response ‚Üí Larger corruption trend ‚Üí Public demand for action.

---

### üåç 6. **Analytical / Policy News**
**Purpose:** Interpret the significance of events or decisions.
**Logical Flow:**
```
> Event summary ‚Üí Expert or analyst insights ‚Üí Supporting data ‚Üí Counter-argument ‚Üí Policy implications ‚Üí Broader conclusion
```
**Example Flow:**
New education policy ‚Üí Expert on implications ‚Üí Data on schools ‚Üí Critic perspectives ‚Üí Economic or social effect ‚Üí Balanced ending.

---

### üí¨ 7. **Opinion / Editorial Piece**
**Purpose:** Persuade or comment.
**Logical Flow:**
```
> Strong position ‚Üí Supporting arguments ‚Üí Evidence/examples ‚Üí Address opposing views ‚Üí Reaffirm argument ‚Üí Call to action / takeaway
```
**Example Flow:**
‚ÄúAI regulation is urgent‚Äù ‚Üí Explain risks ‚Üí Case studies ‚Üí Respond to critics ‚Üí Restate urgency ‚Üí Call for policy action.

---

### üîî 8. **Follow-up / Update Story**
**Purpose:** Advance or deepen a previous news item.
**Logical Flow:**
```
> Reference to earlier event ‚Üí New development ‚Üí Reactions ‚Üí Updated data or consequences ‚Üí Expert analysis ‚Üí Outlook or what‚Äôs next
```
